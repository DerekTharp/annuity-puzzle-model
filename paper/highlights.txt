Highlights

- A nine-channel structural lifecycle model combining six rational channels (Social Security, bequests, combined medical expenditure risk and health-mortality correlation, survival pessimism, pricing loads, inflation erosion) with two preference channels (age-varying consumption needs, state-dependent utility) and one structural channel (public-care aversion) predicts US voluntary annuity ownership of 2.0% - an underestimate of two HRS empirical comparators computed on the same wealth-restricted sample (3.11% lifetime contract indicator, 95% CI [2.54%, 3.81%]; 5.21% any-annuity income proxy, 95% CI [4.45%, 6.09%])
- A complementary reduced-form transport applies the UK 2015 pension-freedoms reform as an external sensitivity check: applying the UK post-reform retention (17% mid; bracket [13%, 25%]) to the frictionless population benchmark of 41.85% yields predicted US ownership of 7.5% (bracket [5.7%, 11.0%]); the conventional HRS income proxy sits just below the bracket's lower edge with its upper 95% confidence bound extending into the bracket
- An exact nine-channel Shapley decomposition over 512 subsets is the disciplined headline attribution; an exploratory eleven-channel extension over 2,048 subsets adds two literature-magnitude behavioral parameters (source-dependent utility, narrow-framing at-purchase penalty) and shows them entering with large but offsetting contributions, illustrative of where standard behavioral parameters would land in the channel ranking but not identified
- Two distinct policy levers operate on different margins: a supply-side reform (group pricing) raises predicted ownership relative to the structural baseline, and a demand-side reform anchored externally by Model 2 operates on a separate margin; neither is a clean structural identification of the underlying mechanism
- The paper does not claim to resolve the puzzle; standard mechanisms account for most of the frictionless-to-observed gap, leaving a small residual that is consistent with the literature-magnitude behavioral channels but is not moment-matched to US data
