Highlights

- Six standard rational channels predict 44.2% ownership relative to a frictionless population benchmark of 41.4%; adding two preference channels (age-varying needs, state-dependent utility) brings the prediction to 34.3%
- Adding source-dependent utility (Force A; Blanchett-Finke spending differential) raises predicted ownership to 79.3%; adding the UK-anchored narrow-framing purchase-event disutility (Force B) yields the bracket [2.3%, 24.5%]
- Both HRS measures of US lifetime annuity ownership are consistent with the model's UK-anchored bracket: 3.34% on the conventional any-annuity income proxy (95% CI [2.89%, 3.85%]) sits inside the bracket; 2.02% on the cleaner fat-file lifetime contract indicator (95% CI [1.68%, 2.43%]) overlaps the bracket's lower edge
- Exact Shapley decomposition over all 1,024 channel subsets attributes ownership reduction without order dependence; the two behavioral channels enter with opposite-sign Shapley values, confirming empirically distinct mechanisms
- Two distinct policy levers: supply-side (group pricing raises ownership) and demand-side (default architecture, psi_purchase = 0, raises ownership to 79.3%) operate on different margins
