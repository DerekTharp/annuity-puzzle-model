1. Summary Assessment
The annuity-pricing component is technically coherent and, in code, mostly implemented the way the manuscript describes. `src/annuity.jl` prices a SPIA off a survival-weighted present value, applies the MWR as a proportional load, and erodes nominal payments in real terms when inflation is active. The manuscript also now includes a real-annuity counterfactual and an inflation-protection sensitivity exercise, which makes the pricing story much more complete than a simple nominal-vs-real comparison.

My main concern is not the algebra, but the empirical framing. The baseline MWR of 0.82 is defensible as a population-table benchmark, yet it is older than the market evidence the paper cites elsewhere, and the headline results are quite sensitive to this choice. The DIA extension is also directionally sensible, but the paper overstates how “similar” DIA and SPIA ownership are, and the welfare column in the DIA table is essentially zero at $200k, so the extension reads more like a robustness illustration than a substantively new result.

3. Specific Weaknesses
- The paper’s claim that the DIA and SPIA ownership rates are “similar” is too strong given the reported numbers. The DIA table shows 7.2% and 7.3% ownership versus 15.8% for the SPIA in the no-bequest case, and 7.9% versus 14.5% under DFJ bequests. That is the same qualitative ranking, but not “similar” in the ordinary sense. See [`paper/main.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/paper/main.tex#L531) and [`tables/tex/dia_comparison.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/tables/tex/dia_comparison.tex#L1).
- The DIA comparison is not a clean product-only experiment because the deferred products are also assigned much worse MWRs, so most of the difference reflects pricing rather than deferral alone. That is economically fine, but the manuscript should say so explicitly and avoid implying that the deferred structure itself is the main driver. See [`paper/appendix.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/paper/appendix.tex#L222) and [`scripts/run_dia_analysis.jl`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/scripts/run_dia_analysis.jl#L29).
- The baseline MWR of 0.82 is plausible, but the justification leans on an older estimate while the paper itself acknowledges more recent values near 0.85 and policy-relevant values near 0.90. Because ownership jumps from 5.3% to 12.5% at 0.85, the headline quantitative fit is fairly load-sensitive. See [`paper/main.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/paper/main.tex#L174) and [`paper/main.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/paper/main.tex#L523).
- The inflation channel is implemented deterministically and elegantly, but the paper still does not fully separate “nominal erosion” from “inflation-indexed annuity pricing” in a way that a pricing specialist would consider complete. The model does a good job with the former; it should be clearer that the latter is a counterfactual product, not simply the inverse of erosion. See [`src/annuity.jl`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/src/annuity.jl#L23) and [`paper/appendix.tex`](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29%20Annuity%20Puzzle%20Simulation/paper/appendix.tex#L116).

4. Concrete Suggestions for Improvement
- Rephrase the DIA discussion to say “lower ownership” or “same broad qualitative pattern,” not “similar ownership,” and add one sentence making clear that the lower DIA MWR is part of the product environment rather than a separate mechanism.
- Add one compact sentence or footnote stating the payment-timing convention for SPIAs, because the current pricing formula assumes an immediate payment stream and readers will otherwise compare it to industry quotes with slightly different conventions.
- Strengthen the MWR calibration by tying the baseline more directly to current quote data or by presenting an age/product-specific range instead of a single fixed load. The existing sensitivity table is useful, but it also makes clear that the 5.3% headline is load-dependent.
- If the goal is to claim that DIA innovation does not materially solve the puzzle, add one counterfactual holding MWR fixed across SPIA and DIA products so the deferral margin is isolated from the pricing margin.
