# Agent 5 Report: Bequest Motive and Wealth Distribution

## 1. Summary Assessment
The DFJ bequest specification is implemented coherently in code, and the manuscript correctly recognizes that `theta` and `kappa` must be interpreted jointly. The paper’s strongest point here is that it does not treat bequests as a generic warm-glow add-on; it actually diagnoses the homothetic mis-specification and shows that the DFJ luxury-good calibration behaves very differently from a small-`kappa` bequest motive.

The main weakness is not the utility formula itself, but the way the decomposition is narrated. The stepwise table makes bequests look quantitatively irrelevant, while the full-model robustness table shows that removing bequests raises ownership from 5.3% to 19.0%. That is not a coding contradiction, but it is a presentation problem: the paper needs to distinguish a marginal stepwise effect from the full-model counterfactual contribution more explicitly.

- The parameter file keeps the bequest primitives centralized and clearly labeled as Lockwood-based defaults in [src/parameters.jl](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/src/parameters.jl#L7).
- The manuscript correctly states that `theta` and `kappa` must be interpreted jointly and explicitly flags the homothetic anomaly when `kappa` is small in [paper/main.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/main.tex#L112).
- The appendix gives a sensible explanation of why the DFJ pair is essentially irrelevant for median-wealth retirees, and it documents the portability check instead of pretending the gamma shift is innocuous in every environment in [paper/appendix.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/appendix.tex#L179).
- The bequest-specification sensitivity table is very valuable because it shows the result is genuinely specification-dependent, not just a mechanical coding artifact; see [tables/csv/bequest_specification_sensitivity.csv](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/tables/csv/bequest_specification_sensitivity.csv#L1).

## 3. Specific Weaknesses
- The manuscript overstates the “zero standalone effect” of bequests without fully reconciling that with the full-model counterfactual. The stepwise decomposition says the bequest step is 100% retention / 0.0 pp in [tables/csv/decomposition.csv](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/tables/csv/decomposition.csv#L4), but the robustness table says “No bequests” implies 19.0% ownership versus 5.3% at the DFJ baseline in [tables/csv/robustness_full.csv](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/tables/csv/robustness_full.csv#L33). The current text in [paper/main.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/main.tex#L114) and [paper/main.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/main.tex#L395) is directionally correct but too compressed for readers to see the difference between marginal and total contribution.
- The portability check is defensible as a sensitivity exercise, but not as strong evidence that `theta = 56.96` is truly portable from `gamma = 2` to `gamma = 2.5`. The appendix acknowledges the ad hoc scaling formula and reports 13.5% divergence, then simply sets a threshold and proceeds in [paper/appendix.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/appendix.tex#L192). That is acceptable as a modeling choice, but the threshold is arbitrary and the implied recalibrated value (`theta = 156.5`) is large enough that the decision deserves a more explicit justification in [paper/main.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/main.tex#L325).
- The `$1` floor in [src/utility.jl](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/src/utility.jl#L24) is harmless under the DFJ baseline, but it means the exact homothetic counterfactual is not literally the analytic form when `kappa` is very small or zero. The paper says the floor “does not bind” in the DFJ case, which is fine, but it should more clearly state that this is a numerical regularization, not part of the economic specification, especially given the homothetic anomaly discussion in [paper/appendix.tex](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/paper/appendix.tex#L181).
- The sensitivity results in [tables/csv/bequest_specification_sensitivity.csv](/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.29 Annuity Puzzle Simulation/tables/csv/bequest_specification_sensitivity.csv#L18) show that alternative `kappa` values can make bequests first-order important. That is a feature, not a bug, but the main text could better distinguish “DFJ luxury-good bequests are weak” from “bequest motives in general are weak.”

## 4. Concrete Suggestions for Improvement
- Add one short paragraph in the results section that explicitly distinguishes three objects: the incremental effect of adding bequests at the decomposition point, the full-model no-bequest counterfactual, and the `kappa` sensitivity exercise.
- Replace the current “zero effect” phrasing with a more precise statement that DFJ bequests have little marginal effect when introduced after SS, but they still matter in the full model through interactions with the pricing and inflation channels.
- Strengthen the portability discussion by reporting the recalibrated `theta` implied by the heuristic formula alongside the chosen baseline, and explain why the authors prefer holding the Lockwood estimates fixed despite the 13.5% divergence.
- State explicitly that the `$1` floor is a numerical safeguard only. If the paper wants to discuss homothetic bequests as a counterfactual, note that the floor slightly regularizes the `kappa = 0` limit.
- Consider adding a leave-one-out or Shapley-style decomposition for bequests versus loads and inflation. That would resolve the current path-dependence concern cleanly and make the “bequests matter in the full model” result easier to interpret.
