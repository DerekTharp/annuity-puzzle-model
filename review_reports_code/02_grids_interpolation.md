# 1. Summary Assessment
The grid/interpolation architecture is conceptually sound, and the spatial grid design is reasonable for the bulk of the sample. The problem is that the current production setup is not fully aligned with the empirical support of the HRS data, and the quadrature evidence does not convincingly justify the chosen production rule. In short: the model is probably fine for mid-range households, but the reported numerical precision is weaker than the paper's headline ownership claims suggest.

# 2. Specific Findings
- **[high] Wealth and annuity grids are too tightly capped for the processed sample, and the code silently clamps out-of-grid households.** `src/parameters.jl:53-60` sets `W_max = 1_000_000.0`, `src/grids.jl:22-27` makes `A_max = p.W_max * payout_rate`, `src/solve.jl:266-269` clamps both `W_rem` and `A_val`, and `src/wtp.jl:541-578` clamps population wealth/annuity income before evaluation. The processed sample contains at least 120 observations above `W_max` (about 2.26% of the unweighted rows and 2.68% of the survey weight), including a 1.3M record at `data/processed/lockwood_hrs_sample.csv:7` and a 38.05M record at `data/processed/lockwood_hrs_sample.csv:32`. Those households are evaluated as if they were sitting exactly at the top grid cell, so the ownership and welfare calculations are not fully supported by the sample support.

- **[high] The quadrature evidence does not support treating `80x30x3x46` with `n_quad=9` as numerically settled.** The appendix says the 9-node rule is the production standard and “well-converged” (`paper/appendix.tex:133-170`), but the actual convergence table shows ownership moving from 21.38% at `80x30, 9-node` to 17.84% at `120x50, 11-node` (`tables/csv/convergence_diagnostics.csv:12-16`). The node sensitivity is even more visible in the raw convergence file, where ownership jumps between 24.56%, 27.92%, 18.37%, 21.38%, and 17.84% as `n_quad` moves from 3 to 11 (`tables/csv/convergence_diagnostics.csv:2-6`). That is a large enough swing that the production quadrature choice should be justified more cautiously before relying on a precise fitted ownership rate.

- **[medium] The interpolation/extrapolation strategy is serviceable inside the grid but weak at the boundaries, and the diagnostics inherit that weakness.** The solver uses piecewise linear interpolation with `Interpolations.Flat()` extrapolation for continuation values (`src/solve.jl:70-75`, `src/solve.jl:188-192`, `src/solve.jl:249-252`). That is stable numerically, but it makes the continuation value locally non-smooth and turns every out-of-range query into a flat top-cell value. The Euler diagnostic then differentiates that interpolant with a fixed finite-difference step (`src/diagnostics.jl:11-15`, `src/diagnostics.jl:82-85`, `src/diagnostics.jl:119-151`), which helps explain why the reported residuals are still around 1.4% on average and above 5% for about 6% of interior points (`tables/csv/euler_residuals.csv:1-8`). This is not a fatal bug, but it is too blunt to be strong validation of the production grid.

# 3. Concrete Fix Recommendations
- Raise `W_max` enough to cover the right tail of the processed HRS sample, or explicitly trim the sample and state the trimming rule in the paper and replication package. If the high-wealth tail is retained, the annuity grid should expand with it rather than being hard-tied to a 1.0-1.1M cap.
- Re-run convergence after increasing the wealth cap and compare at least one higher quadrature rule against the production rule. The current tables show that spatial refinement is fairly tame, but quadrature refinement is still moving the answer by several percentage points.
- Replace the fixed-step derivative check with a derivative calculation tied to the local grid spacing, or at minimum report boundary versus interior residuals separately so the interpolation artifacts are not mixed into the core accuracy statement.
- If flat extrapolation must stay, document clearly that all out-of-bounds households are being evaluated on the boundary cell and quantify how much survey weight lies beyond the grid.
